The yield curve is undergoing a dramatic normalization via a "bear steepener" dynamic. The short end is anchored by Fed easing (Fed Funds down **-86 bps annualized** over 2 years), while the long end is repricing for fiscal dominance and inflation risk.

The **10Y-2Y spread** is moving out of inversion, a classic signal that recession fears are fading and growth/inflation fears are taking hold.
*   **Long-Term (30Y):** The 30Y yield is the canary in the coal mine, surging **+71 bps** over two years and **+20 bps QoQ**.

### 1. MACROECONOMIC, INFLATION EXPECTATIONS & LIQUIDITY TRENDS

The US economy is navigating a precarious "soft-ish" landing characterized by robust nominal growth but deteriorating real liquidity conditions. GDP is expanding at a solid **4.56% CAGR** over the last two years, yet the momentum is stalling; QoQ growth has flattened to **0.00%**, signaling a potential inflection point where high rates finally bite into aggregate demand.

The critical divergence lies in the inflation narrative. While actual CPI has moderated to a **3.29% 2-Year CAGR**, forward-looking expectations are re-anchoring higher. The **1-Year Expected Inflation (EXPINF1YR)** has surged **+23 bps annualized** over the two-year horizon to 3.02%, now trading above the 2-Year and 5-Year expectations. This inversion in the expectations curve (1Y > 2Y > 5Y) suggests the market anticipates a near-term sticky patch before long-term disinflation resumes.

Liquidity is the binding constraint. The Fed Balance Sheet (WALCL) has contracted at a **-3.50% CAGR**, actively draining reserves, while M2 money supply has expanded at a **+4.60% CAGR**. This mismatch indicates that velocity is likely collapsing or that liquidity is trapped in non-bank intermediaries. The combination of shrinking central bank support and rising short-term yields is creating a "liquidity squeeze" that threatens to tip the flat QoQ GDP into contraction.

**Yield Curve Diagnostics:**
*   **30Y - 2Y Spread (Global Liquidity Proxy):** Currently **+86 bps** (5.05% - 4.19%). Historically, a steepening long-end alongside a flat/inverted front-end signals bond vigilantes pricing in fiscal dominance rather than growth.
*   **10Y - 2Y Spread (Policy & Risk):** Currently **+36 bps** (4.55% - 4.19%). The curve has normalized from deep inversion, but the narrow spread offers little premium for duration risk, suggesting caution on rate cuts.
*   **10Y - 3M Spread (Recession Warning):** Currently **+69 bps** (4.55% - 3.86%). The spread is positive, removing the immediate recession signal, but the 3M yield's **-80 bps annualized** decline over two years vs. the 10Y's **+13 bps annualized** rise shows a dramatic shift in policy stance from accommodation to restriction.
*   **30Y - 5Y Spread (Long-Term Curve):** Currently **+251 bps** (5.05% - 2.54%). This extreme steepness reflects a massive term premium demanded for holding long-duration debt amidst rising fiscal deficits (+5.90% CAGR) and unanchored short-term inflation fears.

With wages failing to outpace inflation expectations, affordability is at a breaking point. We are seeing a transition from a supply-constrained market to a demand-constrained one. If unemployment rises another 20-30 bps, this inventory overhang could trigger a sharp correction in home prices, further eroding household wealth and consumer confidence. Main Street is not feeling the "soft landing"; they are feeling the squeeze of sticky prices and expensive credit.
